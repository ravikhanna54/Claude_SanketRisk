Sprinkler System Grading

A structured five-grade sprinkler effectiveness rating based on SRC demerit methodology combined with NFPA 25 deficiency classification. Starting from 100, demerits are applied across water supply, system design, coverage, maintenance, and monitoring — producing a grade that tells the underwriter precisely what credit to apply for sprinkler protection.

1
Fully Effective — Maximum sprinkler credit (90–100)
2
Effective with Minor Deficiencies — Full credit, recs noted (75–89)
3
Partially Effective — Reduced credit, 30–90 day recs required (55–74)
4
Marginally Effective — Minimal credit, immediate referral (35–54)
5
Ineffective / Compromised — No credit — treat as unprotected (<35)

About RSMeans

RSMeans, a division of Gordian, is North America’s leading provider of construction cost data. Trusted by insurers, appraisers, and construction professionals for over 80 years, RSMeans data is the recognized standard for building replacement cost estimation in the insurance industry.
